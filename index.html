Security Tips

1. Avoid these password mistakes:

Avoid trivial passwords like: "god", "letmein" or "master".

Avoid using keyboard patterns like: "12345", "qwerty" or "asdf" as your password.

Avoid simple keyboard character substitutions, e.g. "daisy" = "fsodx".

These passwords are widely overused and are easy to crack.

2. Be mindful of what you publish on your social media


Don't use your pet's name as a password.

Don't use names of your spouse, family members or close friends.

Don't include the date of your birth or birth of your spouse or family members to your password.

Be aware that hackers can observe your social media and learn about you based on information you post there.

3. Best practices for passwords:

Your password can tell a story. Use associations to help you.

Make your password personal, something only YOU would know.

Remember your password as a sentence. The first letter of each word in a sentence is your password. e.g. if your sentence is: "My neighbour's son, Joffrey, was 16 when he got his first Yamaha dirt bike.", than your password would be: "MnsJw16whghfYdb"

4. Manage your passwords:

We can't stress this enought: don't use the same password on multiple accounts.

Be careful where you write down your password.

Use a secure password manager like 1Password or LastPass to store your passwords safely.
